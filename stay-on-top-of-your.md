### The problems

**Last mile breaks the budget and the promise.**  
Costs concentrate at the final mile; first-attempt failures and bad addresses burn fuel, time, and margin — and customers notice before you do.

**Everything is reactive until it hurts.**  
Teams learn about trouble from tickets, angry callers, or SLA breaches — not from the drift in ETA, movement, or route health while there was still room to act.

**Visibility is fragmented; exceptions hide in noise.**  
Carrier updates, dispatch tools, and spreadsheets disagree. Without one timeline of truth, nobody escalates early — they chase status.

**Small incidents cascade.**  
One stalled rider or broken vehicle can blow multiple stops. Static rules fire late; humans discover it even later.

**Trust erodes in silence.**  
When nobody reaches out, customers assume the worst. Proactive communication is rare — so effort and churn spike together.

**Recovery is an afterthought.**  
Restarts, duplicate calls, and lost context turn “one bad delivery” into a long-running mess unless state and decisions survive the chaos.

### Evidence & sources

Industry and research that backs these pain themes (for decks, judges, or footnotes — verify numbers in primaries when you quote them).

**Last mile: cost concentration, failed attempts, addresses, churn**  
Industry roundups and retail logistics analysis describe high **last-mile share of shipping cost**, material **first-attempt failure** rates, **address/data** issues, and **loyalty** hit after failures — e.g. [SmartRoutes — last-mile statistics & insights (2025)](https://smartroutes.io/blogs/last-mile-delivery-statistics-the-complete-data-resource), [Veho — true cost of failed e-commerce deliveries](https://www.shipveho.com/blog/what-is-the-true-cost-of-failed-deliveries-in-e-commerce), and cost-mix context such as [Statista — share of last-mile in total shipping costs](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1434298/last-mile-share-of-total-shipping-costs/). Survey-style industry reports (e.g. logistics leaders on last-mile pressure) appear in vendor research such as [Bringg — State of the Last Mile report](https://go.bringg.com/last-mile-report).

**Breakdowns, congestion, cascade risk on the route**  
Operational commentary ties **vehicle downtime**, **traffic**, and **single-point failures** to multi-stop disruption — e.g. [Oxmaint — last-mile challenges (2026)](https://oxmaint.com/industries/delivery-operations-management/last-mile-challenges-2026), [Spoke Dispatch — expert insights on last mile](https://spoke.com/dispatch/blog/last-mile-delivery-challenges).

**Reactive service; proactive outreach still uncommon**  
Analyst work argues shifting from **reactive** journeys and measuring proactive engagement — [Gartner — organizations should move from reactive to proactive customer service](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2020-10-19-gartner-says-organizations-need-to-move-from-a-reacti) (survey on how rarely customers see proactive service; lifts when they do). Vendor case studies (treat as illustrative, not universal) discuss fewer escalations when teams predict risk — e.g. [SupportLogic — proactive support & escalations](https://www.supportlogic.com/resources/blog/your-guide-to-eliminating-customer-service-burnout-for-good/).

**Fragmented visibility; exception management as a category**  
**Real-time transportation visibility** and **exception management** markets exist precisely because signals are silo’d — see market framing in [Gartner Peer Insights — real-time transportation visibility platforms](https://www.gartner.com/reviews/market/real-time-transportation-visibility-platforms) and problem statements from vendors in that space — e.g. [Princeton TMX — exception management](https://princetontmx.com/our-solution/exception-management), [LogiNext — supply chain visibility & proactive alerts](https://www.loginextsolutions.com/supply-chain-visibility-system).

**Why acting early can beat pure reaction (queueing intuition)**  
Academic work on **proactive service** shows how using **advance information** and spare capacity can reduce delays — [Savva — *Proactive Customer Service* (PDF)](https://prof-savva.github.io/ProactiveCustomerService.pdf); institutional copy: [London Business School research repository](https://lbsresearch.london.edu/id/eprint/1121/1/SavvaN_ProactiveService.pdf).
